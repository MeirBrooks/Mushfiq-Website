Decreasing Deforestation in Brazil

Project Summary

After many years of decline, the rate of deforestation in the Brazilian Amazon increased by 28% between August 2013 and July 2012 (BBC News).  Increased deforestation in Brazil contributes to the loss of biodiversity, changes in water regimes and climate change. Increasing demand for land from farmers and ranchers is one of the main reasons for this increase in the deforestation rate.  Addressing unsustainable practices in ranching and agriculture is one potential way to combat deforestation in this setting.

We plan to conduct a randomized controlled trial in collaboration with Amazon Environmental Research Institute's Sustainable Settlements Project (IPAM-PAS) and the Climate Policy Initiative to evaluate the effects of  providing credit and extension training to rural farmers and ranchers in the Amazon.  Finding effective ways to increase agricultural and ranching productivity without increasing the amount of land employed by these individuals is a promising way to decrease the rate of deforestation in Brazil.
